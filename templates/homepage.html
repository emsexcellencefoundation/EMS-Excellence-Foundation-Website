In San Francisco, California when someone's heart stops beating it takes about two minutes to dispatch emergency services. In Seattle, Washington it takes about about forty seconds. San Francisco's survival rate is 10% and Seattle's rate is 45%. Every minute counts.

Altanta, Georgia used to save no one. After changing its dispatch procedures Altanta saved 33% of patients.
